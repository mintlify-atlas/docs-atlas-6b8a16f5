Carnero.Dev is not just a coding contest — it is a student-driven engineering marathon with a clear mission: build technology that solves real problems. Organized by the TICs career at ITR Roque, the event compresses an entire product development lifecycle into 36 consecutive hours. From problem identification on Friday night to Demo Day pitches on Sunday, every moment is engineered for learning, collaboration, and shipping real software under pressure.

## Mission

The core mission of Carnero.Dev is to empower university students to address genuine community challenges through technology, creativity, and collaboration. The event is deliberately structured to mirror the pressures of real-world software development — tight deadlines, ambiguous requirements, and the need to ship a working product regardless of the obstacles encountered along the way.

Every team that walks through the doors on Friday leaves Sunday with a deployed product, a refined pitch, and a concrete understanding of what it takes to build something that matters. The organizing team — students from the TICs department — believe that the best way to learn software engineering is to build, break, and rebuild under real conditions.

<Note>
  Carnero.Dev is entirely **student-driven and community-supported**. The event is organized, managed, and run by TICs career students at ITR Roque — making it one of the few hackathons in the region where the organizers and participants share the same academic background and institutional environment.
</Note>

## Event Format

Carnero.Dev follows a structured 36-hour continuous development sprint:

- **Start:** Friday, October 16 at 09:00 AM — kickoff ceremony, team formation confirmation, and challenge brief reveal.
- **Development Block:** 36 consecutive hours of uninterrupted building, with live mentoring rounds scheduled every 6 hours to give teams expert guidance and course-correction opportunities.
- **Demo Day:** Sunday morning — all teams present a live demo and pitch of their functional prototype to a judging panel.
- **Team Size:** 1 to 4 members, enrolled in TICs or related university careers.
- **Output:** A fully deployed, functional software product pushed to a public repository.

All code must be original and initiated within the official competition window. Pre-built templates, open-source libraries, and publicly available APIs are permitted, but the core solution must be developed during the event.

## What Participants Get

Every registered team member receives a full competition package designed to maximize focus and productivity throughout the 36-hour sprint:

- **Hack-Packs** — curated participant kits with essential supplies for the competition.
- **Smart Access Badges** — personalized event credentials for entry and identification.
- **Dedicated Workstations** — assigned workspace with power, connectivity, and equipment.
- **Mentor Access** — live mentoring rounds every 6 hours with experienced engineers and domain experts.
- **Food During Development** — catered meals and snacks throughout the entire 36-hour window.
- **Official Certificates** — verified participation and achievement certificates issued to all teams.
- **Networking Opportunities** — direct access to peers, mentors, judges, and industry contacts.

The event identity is built around a terminal-style interface that reflects the engineering culture at its core. The official challenge brief is delivered in a format participants know well:

```bash
$ cat event_rules.txt
> 1. Teams of 1 to 4 members enrolled in TICs or related careers.
> 2. 100% original development started within the hackathon block.
> 3. Deployments ready in production and pushed to public repositories.

$ ./run_hackathon --target_roque

```

```text
BUILD_THE_FUTURE
```

This terminal tagline — `BUILD_THE_FUTURE` — is more than branding. It is the organizing philosophy behind every decision made at Carnero.Dev: every rule, every mentoring session, every judging criterion is designed to push participants closer to building something that could genuinely exist in the world beyond the competition floor.

The Carnero.Dev hackathon spans 3 days filled with coding, mentorship, expert workshops, and high-level competition. From the opening ceremony to the final awards podium, every block of time is intentionally designed to push teams toward their best possible work. Here is the complete timeline so you can plan, prepare, and arrive ready to compete.

## Event Schedule

<Steps>
  <Step title="Registration & Accreditation — Friday Oct 16 · 8:00 AM">
    Distribution of official hack-packs, smart access badges, and workstation assignments. Arrive early to get settled before the opening ceremony kicks off. First-come teams get priority workstation selection.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Opening Ceremony — Friday Oct 16 · 9:30 AM">
    Official kickoff of the Carnero.Dev hackathon. Presentation of mentors and sponsors, and the highly anticipated revelation of track-specific challenges. This is the moment the clock starts — listen closely.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Technology Workshops — Friday Oct 16 · 11:00 AM">
    Express technical sessions on applied AI, rapid deployments, and cybersecurity delivered by industry experts. These workshops are short, dense, and directly actionable — designed to give every team an immediate edge before hacking begins.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Hacking & Mentoring — Friday Oct 16 12:00 PM – Saturday Oct 17 11:59 PM">
    Uninterrupted development. The 36-hour sprint begins the moment workshops close. Technical and business mentoring rounds are held every 6 hours, giving every team the opportunity to pivot strategies, consolidate project scopes, and validate technical decisions with industry professionals.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Demo Day & Pitches — Sunday Oct 18 · 9:00 AM">
    Final project presentations to the judge panel. Each team delivers a 3-minute technical pitch followed by a 2-minute Q&A with the judges. This is where your build speaks — make every second count.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Closing & Awards — Sunday Oct 18 · 2:00 PM">
    Winner announcement, delivery of premium trophies, symbolic checks, and sponsor kits. The ceremony celebrates not just the top three placements but also the special innovation and design recognition awards.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Key Deadlines

All teams must meet the following intermediate checkpoints during the event. Missing a checkpoint does not disqualify a team but may affect mentor availability for that round.

<Note>
  **Idea Validation Checkpoint** — Friday Oct 16, 6:00 PM – 7:30 PM
  Submit your project concept for early mentor feedback. Teams are encouraged to have a defined problem statement and initial technical approach ready.

  **Mid-point Code Demo** — Saturday Oct 17, 11:00 AM – 1:00 PM
  A working prototype or proof-of-concept must be demonstrable to your assigned mentor. This is the primary pivot window — use it.

  **Repository Freeze** — Sunday Oct 18, 8:00 AM (no commits accepted after this time)
  All code, assets, and documentation must be committed and pushed before 8:00 AM on Sunday. Late commits will be excluded from judging.
</Note>
